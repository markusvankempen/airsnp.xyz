SNP Air Quality Project

SNP Air Quality Project exists to give communities access to up to date information about the weeather, and everything related to the safety of their environment.

About Our Project

This project focuses on detecting the ammount of components in the air as well as the humidity and temperature using sensors. It's these sensors that will determine if the air your breathing is clean. On the other hand these sensors can help detect poor air quality environments or even toxic environments. The way were able to know if the air is good or bad is by the led lights on the front panel of the sensor. If glowing green the air is good quality, if red very bad quality, and yellow means poor air quality.

VOCs (Volatile Organic Compounds)

What are VOCs, and Why are They Dangerous?

Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) form a large group of chemicals commonly found in both indoor and outdoor air. For most VOCs, levels found in indoor air in Canadian homes do not usually pose a significant health risk. The reason they are sometimes considered dangerous is because in industrial workers, exposure to high levels of some VOCs has been linked with increased cancer rates. These VOCs include: benzene, formaldehyde. However at the low levels inside your home they pose no threat to your health!

PM2.5s (Particle Matter)

What is PM2.5, and Why do You Care?

PM2.5s are tiny little micro particles that are 3% the size of a human hair! Examples of places these tiny particles are from, are as follows: power plants, motor vehicles, airplanes, residential wood burning, forest fires, agricultural burning, volcanic eruptions and dust storms. Some are emitted directly into the air, while others are formed when gases and particles interact with one another in the atmosphere. Becuase these are such fine particles studies have found a close link between exposure to fine particles and premature death from heart and lung disease. Fine particles are also known to trigger or worsen chronic disease such as asthma, heart attack, bronchitis and other respiratory problems.

Pressure

What is pressure, and Why is it important?

Differences in air pressure are a cornerstone of the changing weather conditions on the planet. The sun's uneven heating of the Earth's surface and ensuing differences in temperature -- essentially where the warm tropics mix with the cold polar regions — give rise to pressure changes. Changes in air pressure could have a big effect on climate. Air pressure controls the atmosphere's circulation, and therefore influences how moisture moves. Changes in circulation can alter rainfall, temperature, winds and storminess.

Temperature

Why is temperature important?

Air temperature affects the growth and reproduction of plants and animals, with warmer temperatures promoting biological growth. Air temperature also affects nearly all other weather phenomena. Temerature plays a role in altering the different pressures in the atmosphere.

Humidity

Why is Humidity important?

Humidity is caused by watervapour in the air combined with warm temperatures. These warm temperatures are thanks to the watervapour. As a greenhouse gas, water vapour lets energy from the Sun pass through and warm the Earth. The Earth then radiates energy away, but some of this energy is trapped on the way out by greenhouse gases which increases the overall temperature or warmth of the environment.
